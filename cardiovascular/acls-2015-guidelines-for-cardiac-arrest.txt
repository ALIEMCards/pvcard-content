# ACLS 2015 Guidelines for Cardiac Arrest

![acls-2015-ca-algorithm.png](images/acls-2015-ca-algorithm.png)

##CPR
- ETCO2 < 10 mm Hg, attempt to improve CPR quality
- If no advanced airway, 30:2 compression-ventilation ratio
- If advanced airway inplace, continuous compressions and 1 breath every 6 seconds.

##Drug Therapy
- <span class="drug">Epinephrine</span> IV/IO dose: 1 mg every 3-5 minutes
- <span class="drug">Amiodarone</span> IV/IO dose: 1st - 300 mg bolus, 2ng - 150 mg bolus

##Advanced Airway
- Endotracheal intubation or subraglottic airway

## Return of Spontaneous Circulation (ROSC)
- Pusle and blood pressure
- Abrupt sustained increase in ETCO2 (typically >/= 40 mm Hg)

## Recersible Causes
- **H**ypovolemia
- **H**ypoxia
- **H**ydrogen ion (acidosis)
- **H**ypo/hyper-kalemia
- **H**ypothermia
- **T**ension pneuomthorax
- **T**amponade, cardiac
- **T**oxins
- **T**hrombosis, pulmonary/coronary

## Indications of considering stopping CPR
- ETCO2 < 10 mm Hg after 20 minutes (*Intubated patients*)
