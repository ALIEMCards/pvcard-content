# Stroke: Contraindications for Thrombolytics

**Inclusion criteria:**
___
*1. Diagnosis of ischemic CVA causing measurable neurologic deficit
2. Age >/= 18 years*

|Time Frame|`Exclusion Criteria`|
|-----------------|:------------------------|
|**0-3 Hours**| 1.) Significant head trauma or prior stroke in previous 3 months|
|from| 2.) Symptoms suggest subarachnoid hemorrhage|
|onset of| 3.) Arterial puncture at noncompressible site in previous  7 days|
|symptoms|4.) History of previous intracranial hemorrhage|
|before| 5.) Intracranial neoplasm, AV malformation, or aneurysm|
|beginning|6.) Recent intracranial or intraspinal surgery|
|treatment| 7.) Elevated BP (SBP > 185 mmHg or DBP > 110)|
|| 8.) Active internal bleeding|
|| 9.) Active bleeding diathesis|
|| -Platlet count < 100,000/mm^3 | 
|| -Heparin recieved within 48 hours, resulting in aPTT above upper limit of normal|
|| -Current use of anticoargulatant with INR > 1.7 or PT > 15 sec|
|| -Current use of direct thrombin inhibitors or direct factor Xa inhibitors with abnormal sensitive labs tests (aPTT, INR, platlet, ecarin clotting time, TT, factor Xa activity assays)|
|| 10.) Blood glucose < 50 mg/dL (2.7 mmol/L)|
|| 11.) CT shows multilobar infarct (hypodensity > 1/3 cerebral hemisphere)|
|**0-3 hours**| `Relative Exclusion Criteria for IV-tPA`(consider risk-benefit ratio)|
|| 1.) Only minor or rapidly improving stroke symptoms|
|| 2.) Pregnancy|
|| 3.) Seizure at onset with postictal residual neurologic impairments|
|| 4.) Major surgery or serious trauma within previous 14 days|
|| 5.) Recent GI or urinary tract hemorrhage within previous 21 days|
|| 6.) Recent acute MI within presious 3 months|
|**3-4.5 hours**| `Exclusion Criteria`|
|| Same exclusion criteria from 0-3 hour time frame above AND any of the following:|
|| 1.) Age > 80 years|
|| 2.) Severe stroke (NIHSS > 25)|
|| 3.) Taking oral anticoagulation regardless of INR|
|| 4.) History of both diabetes AND prior ischemic stroke|
