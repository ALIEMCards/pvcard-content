# Dental Infections

**Dental Caries:** demineralization of protective enamel and subsequent tooth decay 
**Pulpitis:** inflammation of pulp secondary to caries
**Periodontitis:** loss of supportive bone structure caused by chronic gingivitis

![dental-infections.png](images/dental-infections.png)

### Periapical Abscess

- Collection of purulent material at apex of tooth
- Secondary to bacterial invasion from carious destruction of enamel
￼￼￼￼- Hx: Progressive pain, thermal sensitivity
- P/E: Caries, decayed tooth, pain w/ percussion, palpation of apex, gingival swelling, erythema, parulis present, mobile tooth
- Tx:
	1. Antibiotics
		- Uncomplicated: Penicillin or Clindamycin
        - Complicated: <span class="drug">Penicillin</span> + <span class="drug">Metronidazole</span>, <span class="drug">Piperacillin/Tazobactam</span>, or <span class="drug">Clindamycin</span> + <span class="drug">Ceftriaxone</span> – 40% resistance to β lactams in complex odontogenic infections 
	2. Pain control
	3. I+D if abscess present: probe with 18g needle if purulent then 11 blade stab incision hemostat blunt dissection +/- packing
	4. Chlorhexidine 0.1% rinses q2-3h if I + D
	5. Surgical referral, if complicated infxn (Ludwig’s, Lemierre’s Syndrome) 
    
- **Dentist F/U:** Uncomplicated – Generalist 1-2 days, Complicated – Oral Surg ASAP
   
### Periodontal Abscess
- Localized purulent infection within the gingival wall of the periodontal pocket 
- Hx: Swelling, pain, loose tooth
- P/E: Purulent discharge, erythema, fluctuant mass, dental extrusion
- Tx: 
	1. Pain Control – dental block, NSAID’s +/- opioids
	2. I + D abscess prn: 11 blade stab incision and hemostat blunt dissection +/- packing
	3. Antibiotics: <span class="drug">Penicillin </span>or <span class="drug">Clindamycin</span>
	4. <span class="drug">Chlorhexidine</span> 0.1% rinses q2-3h
    
- **Dentist F/U:** Generalist – 1-2 days
   
### Pericoronitis
- Inflammation +/- infection surrounding impacted or partially erupted tooth
￼￼￼￼- Hx: Usually 3rd molar (Wisdom Tooth), erupting teeth, pain, swelling, halitosis 
- P/E: Erythema, swelling, +/- abscess
- Tx: Same as for <b>Periodontal Abscess</b>
- **Dentist F/U:** Generalist in 1-2 days; definitive treatment – Oral Surgery
